### Definition

Hladni val je razdoblje značajnog i neuobičajenog hladnog vremena, obilježeno oštrim i značajnim padom temperature (maksimalna, minimalna i prosječna dnevna temperatura, °C) na površini, na velikom području i trajanje ispod određenih graničnih uvjeta najmanje dva uzastopna dana tijekom hladne sezone (WMO, 2020).

- WMO (Svjetska meteorološka organizacija), 2020. *Vodič za definiciju i praćenje ekstremnih vremenskih i klimatskih događaja*. Pristupeno 16. svibnja 2025.

### Annotations

#### Additional scientific description

U Sjedinjenim Američkim Državama, Američka nacionalna meteorološka služba definira hladni val kao brzi pad temperature unutar 24 sata na temperature koje zahtijevaju znatno povećanu zaštitu za poljoprivredu, industriju, trgovinu i društvene aktivnosti. Kriterij za hladni val je dvostruk: tempo pada temperature i minimalna temperatura na koju padne. Drugi faktor ovisi o regiji i dobu godine (AMS, 2019). U Kini, Kineska meteorološka administracija definira hladni val kao katastrofalno vrijeme zimi. Hladni zrak koji dolazi s visokih širina brzo se jača pod posebnim vremenskim uvjetima kada ulazi u srednje i niske širine, čime se uzrokuje oštar pad temperature, gale, snijeg i kiša. Kada se hladni zrak na jugu dostigne određeni standard, postaje hladni val (Kineska meteorološka administracija, 2012). Hladni val se ne smije miješati s *hladnim periodom*, koji se odnosi na trajno iznadprosječne temperature tijekom toplih mjeseci, a koji također može imati ozbiljan utjecaj na društvo, posebno na ljudsko zdravlje i poljoprivredu (WMO, 2020).

#### Metrics and numeric limits

- **Indeks**: Dnevne vrijednosti Tmax, Tmin i/ili prosječne temperature. Drugi indeks može se izračunati korištenjem promjene temperature u 24 sata prije početka događaja.
- **Prag**: Određuje se na temelju povijesnih vrijednosti indeksa.
- **Temporalni**: Informacije na razini stanica o datumu početka, datumu završetka i trajanju događaja. Hladni val traje najmanje dva dana.
- **Prostorni**: Izračunajte pogođeno područje, pružajući postotak stanica gdje je prag prelazio; locirajte koordinate pogođenih stanica i centar s najvišim/najnižim vrijednostima indeksa; opcionalno, ali preporučeno ako su resursi dostupni, koristite GIS (geografski informacijacijski sustav) za izračun područja pogođenog događajem, jačine i ozbiljnosti.

### Impacts

Human health impacts from cold waves include mortality from ischaemic heart disease and cerebrovascular disease, both of which increase in cold weather. An increase in respiratory disease is generally attributed to cross‑infection from indoor crowding and the adverse effects of cold on the immune system’s resistance to respiratory infection, as well as to the fact that low temperatures assist survival of bacteria in droplets (Eurowinter Group, 1997). Living in a cold house can affect health at any age, not just in old age, for a variety of reasons. Although the extra deaths in elderly people are caused mainly by cardiovascular and respiratory disease, far greater numbers have minor ailments that lead to a huge burden of disease, costs to the health system, and misery. Compared with those who live in a warmer house, respiratory problems are roughly doubled in children, arthritis and rheumatism increase, and mental health can be impaired at any age. Adolescents who live in a cold house have a five‑fold increased risk of multiple mental health problems (Dear & McMichael, 2011).

### Risk Management

As an example, the Cold Weather Plan for England first launched in 2011 helps prevent the major avoidable effects on health during periods of cold weather in England (UK Government, 2020). National alerting parameters for Cold Wave Warning are available in China (Kineska meteorološka administracija, 2012).

The section above and the table below offer an overview of monitoring cold waves. This information can be used for forecasting within a national early warning system (EWS). | Meteorological services | Tracking temperature drops, wind chill, extreme cold conditions |

| Energy and utility companies | Preparing for increased heating demand, power‑grid strain, potential outages due to extreme cold |

| Health authorities | Evaluating public health risks: hypothermia, frostbite, respiratory illnesses, emergency medical response needs |
| Researchers and academics | Studying cold wave formation, climate change, forecasting improvements |

### How is the Hazard Observed/Monitored/Forecast?

Cold waves are monitored using satellites and weather stations with temperature sensors to track sudden drops in temperature, wind chill, and atmospheric pressure changes. Meteorologists analyse polar air movements, jet stream shifts, moisture levels, and advanced weather models to predict extreme cold events, enabling forecasters to issue early warnings. These alerts assist communities, energy providers, and emergency responders in preparing for health risks, heating demands, and potential disruptions to infrastructure and transportation.
